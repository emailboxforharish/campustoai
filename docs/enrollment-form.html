Student Details

This Agreement is entered into between CampusToAI Academy ("Academy", "We", "Us") and the student named above ("Student", "You"). By enrolling and making payment, the Student agrees to be legally bound by all terms and conditions set forth in this document. This Agreement governs the enrollment, fees, refund policy, schedule, placement support, conduct, and all related matters pertaining to the Programme enrolled in by the Student ("Programme"). Please read this document in its entirety before signing.

1. Programme Details
1.1 The Programme enrolled in by the Student is as communicated by the Academy at the time of admission and is offered exclusively in a live online format via video conferencing platforms designated by the Academy.
1.2 The Programme duration, curriculum, and subject matter are as communicated to the Student during the admission process. The Academy reserves the right to make reasonable adjustments to curriculum content without prior notice.
1.3 Each batch is limited in size to ensure individualised mentorship. The batch size is determined by the Academy at its sole discretion.
1.4 Classes will be conducted live online. Session timings will be communicated prior to batch commencement and are subject to the scheduling terms in Section 5.
1.5 A Certificate of Completion will be issued upon successful completion of the Programme, including minimum attendance and project submission requirements as defined in Section 7.
2. Enrollment & Seat Confirmation
2.1 Enrollment is confirmed only upon receipt of the full Programme fee as communicated in the admission offer letter, unless a payment plan has been separately agreed in writing by the Academy.
2.2 Seats are allocated on a first-come-first-served basis. Payment of fee does not guarantee enrollment if the batch is already full; in such cases the full fee will be refunded within 7 working days.
2.3 The Academy reserves the right to reject an enrollment application if the applicant does not meet the minimum prerequisite criteria communicated during the admission process.
2.4 Enrollment is personal and non-transferable. The seat cannot be transferred to another individual.
3. Fee & Payment Terms
3.1 The Programme fee is as stated in the admission offer letter issued to the Student. This fee covers live instruction, recorded session access (where made available), project materials, mock interview sessions, resume review, and placement support as described in Section 6.
3.2 The fee does not cover personal hardware, internet connection charges, third-party software licenses, or cloud platform credits beyond those specifically provided by the Academy.
3.3 Where an installment plan is agreed in writing, failure to pay any installment within 5 days of the due date may result in suspension of access to classes. Access will be restored upon payment of the outstanding amount.
3.4 Fee receipts will be issued via email within 2 working days of confirmed payment.
4. No Refund Policy
All fees paid to the Academy are non-refundable. Once enrollment is confirmed by the Academy, no refund will be issued for any reason.
5. Batch Schedule & Rescheduling
The batch schedule is subject to rescheduling. The Academy reserves the right to reschedule sessions, batch timings, or the batch start date at its discretion. The Academy will notify the Student in advance of any such changes.
6. Placement Support
What Placement Support Includes: Resume review & LinkedIn optimisation, GitHub portfolio review, 2 mock technical interview sessions, 1 HR mock interview session, job referrals to the Academy's hiring network, and post-placement follow-up guidance.
6.1 Placement support is provided as a best-effort service and is not a guarantee of employment. The Academy does not guarantee job placement, salary, or interview selection at any specific company.
6.2 Placement support commences only upon full completion of the Programme and is available to students who have met the attendance and project submission requirements in Section 7.
6.3 Students who have outstanding fees or installments are not eligible for placement support until all dues are cleared.
6.4 The Academy will provide placement support for a period of up to 3 months after Programme completion. After 3 months, placement assistance is at the Academy's discretion.
6.5 Placement support is conditional on active student participation. The Academy may discontinue support if the student:
  • Does not respond to communication within 48 hours repeatedly;
  • Refuses to attend scheduled mock interviews without prior notice;
  • Declines reasonable job referrals without valid justification on more than 2 occasions;
  • Misrepresents their skills or experience to prospective employers.
6.6 Any salary figures communicated in Academy marketing materials are indicative market ranges based on available data and are not guaranteed outcomes. Actual salary offers depend on the student's performance, the hiring company, location, and market conditions.
6.7 Job referrals are introductions only. The Academy does not participate in or influence the hiring decision of any company. Selection, rejection, and offer terms are entirely at the discretion of the respective employer.
6.8 The student agrees to notify the Academy within 5 days of receiving any job offer, whether sourced through the Academy's network or independently, so that the Academy may maintain accurate placement records.
7. Attendance & Completion Requirements
7.1 Students are expected to attend a minimum of 80% of scheduled live sessions. Attendance below 80% may result in ineligibility for the Completion Certificate and placement support.
7.2 Students must complete and submit the Final Capstone Project within 7 days of the last scheduled class session to be eligible for the Completion Certificate.
7.3 Students are expected to be punctual. Joining a session more than 15 minutes late without prior notice may be marked as absent for that session.
7.4 The Academy reserves the right to withhold the Completion Certificate if the student has outstanding fees or has violated the Code of Conduct (Section 8).
8. Student Code of Conduct
8.1 Students must maintain respectful, professional conduct in all live sessions, group chats, and communications with instructors, staff, and fellow students.
8.2 The following are strictly prohibited and may result in immediate termination of enrollment without refund:
  • Harassment, bullying, or discriminatory behaviour towards any person;
  • Sharing, distributing, or uploading Academy class recordings, materials, or content to any third party or public platform;
  • Academic dishonesty including submission of work that is not the student's own;
  • Attempting to circumvent or misuse Academy systems or platforms;
  • Recording of live sessions without explicit written permission from the Academy.
8.3 Students are required to maintain a functioning camera and microphone for live sessions. The Academy may require cameras to be on during key interactive sessions.
9. Intellectual Property
9.1 All course content, materials, session recordings, slides, code samples, and curriculum developed by the Academy are the exclusive intellectual property of CampusToAI Academy and are protected under applicable Indian copyright law.
9.2 Students are granted a limited, personal, non-transferable, non-exclusive licence to use course materials solely for personal learning purposes during and after the Programme.
9.3 Projects built by students during the Programme using the Academy's framework remain the student's intellectual property. The Academy may, with the student's written consent, use anonymised project descriptions for marketing and promotional purposes.
10. Privacy & Data
10.1 The Academy collects and processes student personal data (name, contact, educational background) solely for the purposes of delivering the Programme, providing placement support, and issuing certificates.
10.2 Student data will not be sold to third parties. Data may be shared with prospective employers as part of the placement referral process, with the student's implied consent by enrolling.
10.3 The Academy may request permission to use the student's name, testimonial, and placement outcome in marketing materials. Participation is entirely voluntary and does not affect any Academy services.
11. Limitation of Liability
11.1 The Academy's total liability to the student under this Agreement, in any circumstance, shall not exceed the total Programme fee paid by the student.
11.2 The Academy is not liable for any indirect, consequential, or special damages including loss of employment opportunity, salary, or business arising from the student's use of or inability to use Academy services.
11.3 The Academy is not responsible for disruptions caused by third-party platforms (Zoom, Google Meet, etc.), internet outages, or force majeure events including pandemics, natural disasters, or government-imposed restrictions.
12. Dispute Resolution & Governing Law
12.1 In the event of a dispute, the parties agree to first attempt resolution through good-faith discussion within 15 days of the dispute being raised in writing.
12.2 If the dispute cannot be resolved through discussion, it shall be referred to a sole arbitrator mutually agreed upon by both parties, in accordance with the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 (India).
12.3 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India. The courts of Pune, Maharashtra shall have exclusive jurisdiction over all disputes arising under this Agreement.

13.1 The Academy reserves the right to amend these terms and conditions. Material amendments will be communicated to enrolled students in writing at least 7 days before taking effect. Continued participation in the Programme constitutes acceptance of the amended terms.
